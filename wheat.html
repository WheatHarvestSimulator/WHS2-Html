Character Creation

Top

Stats are as follows:

  1. Speed

    Governs movement, intiative, and dodge chance.

  2. Power

    Your physical strength and ability to deal damage with weapons.

  3. Charisma

    Your ability to speak to people and be listened to.

  4. Intelligence

    Equal to magic points per day, and reflects your general intelligence. Arcane Magic comes from intelligence.

  5. Endurance

    Your health.

  6. 6th Sense

    Your almost unnatural intuition. Death Magic, Shadow Magic, and magic based on detection comes from your 6th Sense.

Generating Stats

Players have the option of assigning each stat with either:

  • 25, 30, 30, 35, 40, 45

Or

  • 10, 20, 30, 40, 50, 60

Skills

Pick 8 skills for your character. These can be combat related, magic related, or anything at all. Skills are based on percentages: of the 8 skills selected, choose 4 to start with 30%. The remaining skills will start at 15%.

Leveling

When a character gains a level

  1. 5 points are given to distribute to Stats
  2. 16 points are given to distribute to Skills, with a max addition of 10% to any one skill

Actions

Top

In Wheat Harvest Simulator 2, all gameplay including roleplay takes place inside a turn structure, during which players can perform approximately one action per turn.

An action consists of doing something that requires a dice roll or would consume the in-game time allotted to one turn. Swinging a weapon, casting a spell, fleeing a pursuer, or drinking a regular-sized potion are all actions.

Some things, like running a short distance, drawing an arrow from a quiver, or letting out a battle cry don't use a full action. This is why turns are approximately one action long - they're usually one action and some change.

There is no fixed turn duration, rather, a turn's duration changes based on the pacing of the scene. A turn during combat might be 6 seconds or less, whereas a turn in a stealth or travel scene may be anywhere from 5 minutes to an hour.

Characters including players take their turns in descending order of their Speed, but players with higher speed can choose to delay their turns until later in the initiative stack. For example, a nimble thief may want to use his turn to react to something a slower character does, such as interrupting a spellcaster.

To take an action you have a skill related to, roll 1d20 + (Skill/10). For instance, a thief picking a lock with a lockpicking skill of 54% would roll 1d20 and add 5.4 to the result.

If you have 2 or more skills that affect the same thing (e.g. battleaxes and two-handed weapons) the higher skill is the one applied.

When attempting an action without a corresponding skill, instead use its corresponding core stat divided by 20.

Combat

Top

Initiative = Speed
If speeds are tied, flip a coin to decide order

Endurance = Health


Permanent damage to Endurance occurs at 0 points
Death occurs at -20 points

Attacking:

Roll one D20 and add the percentage of the combat skill

Example:

A 30% long blade skill adds +3 to hit

Dodging/Armor

Dodge = 10 + SPEED/10

Armor:

  1. NONE = -0 TO SPEED
  2. LIGHT = -10 TO SPEED
  3. MED = -20 TO SPEED
  4. HEAVY = -30 TO SPEED

Characters in medium or heavy armor without using a shield gain an additional -5 penalty to dodge chance.

Damage

Damage = POWER/10 + 1 point for every 20% of the weapon skill +

DAMAGE ARMOR TYPE
D20 NONE
D10 LIGHT
D8 MED
D6 HEAVY

Special Attacks

If SPEED > 60, then attack twice, the second roll will be the next roll in the table

Example:

The opponent is wearing light armor, and you hit. Roll 1D10 and 1D8 for damage

If the character is wearing Heavy armor, roll an additional D6

If Duel Wielding or using a Two Handed weapon, attack twice regardless of speed, using the same descending order

If SPEED > 60 AND duel wielding/two handed, roll the same die twice

Grappling:

Check = (POWER+SPEED)/10 + D10

Both characters do the check and the higher number determines who wins the attempt

Magic

Top

Your maximum MP is equal to your Intelligence, and your MP is set to maximum after you rest.

Taking a short break without committing to a full night's sleep restores a maximum of (Intelligence/4) MP, but only once between full rests.

EASY 5

Casting a spell is an action, like any other. Most spells are marked with their cost (MP) and their difficulty (see table). Some spells are rituals, which have no MP cost but take a long time to cast.

Skills specializing in certain types of magic are also added to the cast check in the same way as any other skill check. When a spell asks for (YOUR ROLL), it means 1d20+(Skill modifier of that school).

Casting two of the same spell that changes a character's stats on the same target only reduces their stats once. Buffs or debuffs DO stack with different buffs or debuffs, but not with themselves.

Equipment

Carrying capacity = POWER + ENDURANCE + 20
